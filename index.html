Calculation Theory & Formula Breakdown

1 Resistance (DC & AC Limits)

Includes copper resistivity calculations against physical area trace lengths. Used to construct the baseline series impedance before high-frequency AC behaviors.

$$R_{dc} = \rho_{cu} \frac{L_{total}}{A_{cross}}$$
Variables:
  • \(\rho_{cu}\): \(1.68 \times 10^{-8} \, \Omega \cdot m\)
  • \(L_{total}\): Geometric unwound spiral length
  • \(A_{cross}\): Trace Width \(\times\) Thickness

    • 2 Planar Inductance (Wheeler)

    Modified Wheeler Formula accurately mapping square/rectangular planar spiral fields mathematically.

    $$L = \frac{\mu_{0} N^{2} d_{avg} c_{1}}{2} \Big[ \ln(\frac{c_{2}}{\rho}) + c_{3}\rho + c_{4}\rho^{2} \Big]$$
    Variables:
  • \(\mu_{0}\): Vacuum permeability (\(4\pi \times 10^{-7}\))
  • \(\rho\): Coil Fill Ratio \((D_{out} - D_{in}) / (D_{out} + D_{in})\)
  • \(C_{flex}\): \(C_1=1.27, C_2=2.07, C_3=0.18, C_4=0.13\)
    • Ref: S. Mohan, "Accurate Planar Spiral Inductance" (IEEE 1999)

    3 Quality Factor (Q)

    Dictates bandwidth efficiency. MRD2 needs Q between 10-20 to support FDX bandwidth constraints while maintaining high driving efficiency.

    $$Q = \frac{X_{L}}{R} \quad \implies \quad Q = \frac{2 \pi f L}{R}$$
    Constraints:
  • \(f\): Target Operating Frequency (134.2 kHz)
  • \(X_{L}\): Target \(47 \mu\text{H} \approx 39.6 \, \Omega\)
  • Because \(Q\) targets \(10 \to 20\), Resistance MUST fall internally tracking \( \approx 2.0 \to 4.0 \, \Omega \).
