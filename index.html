Calculation Theory & Formula Breakdown

Step 1 Base Physicals & DC Resistance

The total length of the spiral is estimated using the geometry of each wrap. The DC resistance is directly derived from standard Copper resistivity laws.

$$R_{dc} = \rho_{cu} \frac{L_{total}}{A_{cross}} = \rho_{cu} \frac{L_{total}}{W_{tr} \times T_{cu}}$$
Variable References:
  • \(\rho_{cu}\): Resistivity of Copper (\(1.68 \times 10^{-8} \, \Omega \cdot m\))
  • \(L_{total}\): Total trace length (perimeters minus turning deductions).
  • \(A_{cross}\): Cross section (Trace Width \(\times\) Copper Weight Thickness). 1oz Cu = 35µm.
    • Ref: Standard Material Resistivity Equations.

    Step 2 Rectangular Planar Inductance

    The Modified Wheeler Formula is an industry standard designed by Mohan et al. (Stanford University) highly accurate for calculating square/rectangular planar spiral coil inductance on PCBs.

    $$L = \frac{\mu_{0} N^{2} d_{avg} c_{1}}{2} \Big[ \ln(\frac{c_{2}}{\rho}) + c_{3}\rho + c_{4}\rho^{2} \Big]$$
    Variable References:
  • \(\mu_{0}\): Vacuum permeability (\(1.256 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{H/m}\)).
  • \(\rho\): Fill Ratio. \( \rho = (D_{out} - D_{in}) / (D_{out} + D_{in}) \). Dictates how hollow the center of the coil is.
  • \(C_{1 \to 4}\): Shape Constants. For Rectangular spirals: \(C_1=1.27, C_2=2.07, C_3=0.18, C_4=0.13\).
    • Ref: S. Mohan et al., "Simple Accurate Expressions for Planar Spiral Inductances" (IEEE 1999).

    Step 3 The Quality Factor (Q)

    The Q-factor defines the efficiency and bandwidth of the antenna. The TI Texas Instruments Reader strictly requires a Q factor between 10 to 20 for HDX glass tag FDX burst reading. If Q>20, bandwidth narrows and the 134.2 kHz receiver gets clipped. If Q<10, the trace resistance limits the transmission power.

    $$X_{L} = 2 \pi f L \quad \longrightarrow \quad Q = \frac{X_{L}}{R + R_{reader}}$$
    Variable References:
  • \(f\): Target Operating Frequency (134,200 Hz).
  • \(X_{L}\): Inductive Reactance at the operating frequency.
    • TI Specific Limits:
  • TI RI-STU-MRD2 Reader Target: \(L = 47 \, \mu\text{H}\).
  • Therefore: \(X_{L} \approx 39.6 \, \Omega\) at 134.2 kHz.
  • To keep \( Q \approx 10 \to 20 \), resistance \(R\) must be roughly 2.0 to 4.0 Ohms.
